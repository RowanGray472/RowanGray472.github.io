Harira

Background

Harira is a traditional North African soup often prepared in Morocco and Algeria! It's a delicious healthy meal and tastes especially nice on a cold winter day. It is often served with bread but works great on its own as well.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 stalks of celery, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons dried ground ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 2 teaspoons cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 6 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • pinch of saffron
  • 1-2 teaspoons sea salt
  • 1/2 cup dry red lentils
  • 1/2 cup dry brown or green lentils
  • 1 1/2 cups chickpeas, cooked
  • 1/4 pound capellini pasta
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1/2 cup parsley

Instructions

  1. Sauté onions for 5 minutes in olive oil, over medium heat.
  2. Add celery, garlic, ginger, pepper, turmeric, cumin, smoked paprika and cayenne. Stir for another minute. Sautéing the spices allows them to bloom and deepen in flavor.
  3. Add broth, tomatoes, cinnamon sticks,saffron, salt, red lentils, brown lentils, half of the cilantro and half of the parsley. Bring to a simmer, cover with a vented lid, for 30 minutes.
  4. Add chickpeas and pasta and cook for 5-10 minutes
  5. Add remaining fresh herbs
